Abstract

Reconstructing 3D scenes from a single RGB image remains a core challenge in computer graphics and vision. Existing methods often struggle to simultaneously ensure the quality of individual assets and the coherence of the overall scene in complex scene generation tasks. To address these limitations, we propose XXX, a novel system that leverages the prior knowledge of foundational vision large models to accomplish both single image-to-3D scene reconstruction and texture editing in a zero-shot manner. For 3D scene generation, XXX decouples foreground and background elements, extracts object-level 2D segmentation and depth information from the input image, and employs point cloud-guided object pose optimization to achieve more accurate scene alignment. Furthermore, XXX supports texture editing for objects within a scene. We impose constraints on diffusion models and introduce a mask-guided progressive image generation strategy, effectively maintaining multi-view texture consistency. By further integrating PBR material estimation, we enhance the realism of rendered outputs. Experimental results demonstrate that our framework not only ensures the accuracy of generated assets, but also faithfully reconstructs scene layouts and produces highly detailed textures that closely align with text prompts. Leveraging generative artificial intelligence, XXX can transform 2D images into diversified 3D worlds with various styles, showing broad application potential in virtual content creation, such as digital twinning and immersive game production. Moreover, in the field of robotics, the system can effectively support "real-to-sim" transfer and generate highly realistic and trainable simulation environments.

3D Scene Generation

Overview of 3D scene generation. XXX renders geometrically-aware conditions (including normal, position, and edge maps) of a given mesh from the initial camera viewpoint, which are then injected into a diffusion model along with a text prompt. After obtaining a single-view image aligned with the geometric structure, a mask-guided progressive image generation strategy is employed to synthesize a sequence of RGB images with multiview consistency. The resulting image set is preprocessed with lighting elimination and super-resolution before being mapped back onto the mesh surface via a back projection module.
